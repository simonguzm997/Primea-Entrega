Who am I?

My name is Simón Guzmán, I am 23 years old and live in Bogota. I was born in Armenia in 1997 but was raised in Cali. My parents divorced when I was very young, so I grew up with my mom but would visit my dad at least once a week. When I was around 9 years old, my mom married an Italia guy called Dario. Due to the marriage, I spent most of my vacations in Italy and was finally able to see snow when I was there.

I’ve had animals all my life, but everything started with dogs. Before I was born, my mom had two dogs called Simba and Chia. Then, when I was very little, Chia had puppies and we kept one of them naming it Bruno. Some years later, while taking Bruno to the park, we saw a Husky and I loved it so much, I asked my mom from one. When she arrived, we called her Luna and she was a white fluffy ball. A couple years ago, Dario talked to a guy who had a Samoyedo called Nicolas and was going to be abandoned, so we adopted it. Nicolas and Luna had four puppies, we gave three away and kept one I called Polinesio. Finally, a year ago my mom adopted a dog called Lula who was being abused by her owner.

Simultaneously, I had lots of other pets. I had a hamster called Manchita who escaped her cage every night. I had tree bunnies, sadly two of them dies very fast but the third one bit my thumb once. I also had two canaries, one blue and one green that my aunt gave me as she had a ton of them. Then, at an event in my school I won a chick, sadly it dies in a couple of weeks. Now days, I have three cats. I adopted Pelusa and Selena from a vet close to where I live as they where brother and sister, I decided not to separate them. Finally, around one year later, I found a cat at the universities parking lot. I asked around to see if she had an owner and no one knew about it, so I took her home. Took her to the vet and she had no health issues. I put posters in the area where I found her, with pictures and my number, in case her owner would appear, but no one called so I kept her.

Now days, I’m studying computer engineer at Universidad de los Andes. I hope to finish the career next year and start master’s in security, as it’s the field that I love the most.
